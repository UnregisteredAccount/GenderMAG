About CultureMag

From manual
framework to digital
platform

CultureMag was originally developed as a manual heuristic evaluation tool to identify culture-related usability issues in digital systems used for parent–teacher communication. It surfaced mismatches in communication style, assumptions about parental roles, language barriers, and expectations around authority.

This project transforms that framework into an interactive digital platform — embedding its cultural dimensions, evaluation criteria, scoring structure, and reporting mechanisms into a consistent, scalable tool that any researcher, administrator, or designer can use.

Evaluation dimensions

Six lenses for
cultural usability

Each dimension captures a distinct axis of cultural friction that platforms may embed — knowingly or not.

01
🗣
Communication style
Direct vs. indirect norms, formality registers, turn-taking expectations, and tone assumptions embedded in platform design.
02
👨‍👩‍👧
Parental role assumptions
Who is presumed the primary caregiver, how household structures are accommodated, and what engagement patterns are expected.
03
🌐
Language accessibility
Multilingual support quality, reading level assumptions, translation completeness, and access for non-native speakers.
04
🏫
Authority & hierarchy
Power distance in teacher–parent framing, institutional deference cues, and the balance of voice in platform design.
05
🤝
Engagement expectations
Assumed participation modes, digital literacy levels, notification defaults, and obligations placed on parents to respond.
06
✦
Cultural representation
Imagery defaults, naming conventions, calendar and holiday assumptions, and symbolic choices embedded throughout the UI.
